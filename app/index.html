Lincoln Water Usage & Flows in the Platte

Each summer, water use increases in Lincoln, often doubling from winter rates as residents turn on lawn sprinklers, water gardens and wash their cars. This is especially true in drought years, particularly in 2002 and 2012, despite Lincoln Water System's restrictions on water use those years.

By contrast, flow rates on the Platte River at the well field near Ashland vary greatly, affecting the amount of water available to the city. This is particularly evident in the last five years, when flows ranged from 50,000 cfs in 2010 to 500 cfs in 2012.

Still, average per capita use has declined by about 25 percent in the past 30 years as population increased nearly the same amount, meaning that Lincoln's annual average water use holds steady to those from the early 1970s.
